Defect-Limited Carrier Mobility from the $T$-matrix

Two routes from the machinery of this site to electron/hole mobility: (A) the Boltzmann transport equation with scattering rates from the on-shell imaginary self-energy, and (B) the Kubo formula evaluated directly on the disorder-averaged spectral function $A(k,\omega)$. Formulas with derivation sketches, now implemented through all three Route-A rungs and cross-validated against the Route-B bubble (§E).

0 · Setup: from one impurity to a dilute random array

Everything on this site so far is single-impurity: the vertex $\tilde V(k_i,k_f)=W_{PP}+W_{PQ}[\omega_0-H_{QQ}]^{-1}W_{QP}$ (A1-augmented, artifact-free) and the torus $T$-matrix $T(k,k';\omega)=\big[1-\tilde V G^0(\omega)\big]^{-1}\tilde V$ on the $N_k$-cell supercell. Transport needs the disorder-averaged Green function for a random array of identical defects with concentration $c$ per primitive cell (areal density $n_{\rm imp}=c/A_{\rm cell}$; our supercells correspond to $c=1/36$). To lowest order in $c$ (dilute, non-overlapping impurities — the standard non-crossing $T$-matrix approximation of disorder theory):

$$\boxed{\;\Sigma_{n}(k,\omega)\;=\;c\,N_k\,T_{nk,\,nk}(\omega)\;+\;\mathcal O(c^2)\;}$$

Our torus $T$ scales as $1/N_k$ (because $\tilde V=M/N_k$), so $c\,N_k\,T$ is finite and $N_k$-independent in the thermodynamic limit — the combination $N_kT$ is the physical per-impurity scattering amplitude, and $c$ counts impurities. The averaged retarded Green function and spectral function are then

$$\bar G^R_{n}(k,\omega)=\frac{1}{\omega-\varepsilon_{nk}-\Sigma_n(k,\omega)},\qquad A_n(k,\omega)=-\tfrac1\pi\,{\rm Im}\,\bar G^R_n(k,\omega) =\frac{1}{\pi}\,\frac{\Gamma_{nk}(\omega)/2}{\big[\omega-\varepsilon_{nk}-{\rm Re}\,\Sigma\big]^2+\big[\Gamma_{nk}(\omega)/2\big]^2},$$

with the (energy-dependent) broadening $\Gamma_{nk}(\omega)=-2\,{\rm Im}\,\Sigma_n(k,\omega)\ge0$. Two immediate connections to what is already computed: (i) the maps of §9 of the Augmentation page are exactly $A_n(k,\omega)$ at $c=1/N_k$ (one impurity per torus) plus a visualization broadening $\eta$; (ii) the linewidth of a quasiparticle peak in those maps, minus $\eta$, is the scattering rate scaled to that concentration. For transport the $\eta$ used for plotting must never enter — only the physical $\Gamma$ does.

A · Route 1: Boltzmann transport with $T$-matrix rates

A.1  On-shell rate: "Im $\Sigma$ = scattering rate × factor"

The quasiparticle (out-scattering) lifetime follows from evaluating $\Sigma$ on shell, $\omega\to\varepsilon_{nk}$:

$$\boxed{\;\frac{1}{\tau^{\rm qp}_{nk}}\;=\;\frac{2}{\hbar}\,\big|{\rm Im}\,\Sigma_n(k,\varepsilon_{nk})\big| \;=\;\frac{2\,c\,N_k}{\hbar}\,\big|{\rm Im}\,T_{nk,nk}(\varepsilon_{nk}+i0^+)\big|\;}$$

— the "factor" is precisely $2/\hbar$ (lifetime vs. amplitude decay) together with the concentration $cN_k$ hidden inside $\Sigma$. The generalized optical theorem turns the diagonal Im $T$ into an explicit golden rule with the full $T$ replacing the bare vertex (all scattering orders, exact for one impurity):

$${\rm Im}\,T_{nk,nk}(\varepsilon+i0^+)=-\pi\sum_{n'k'}\big|T_{nk,\,n'k'}(\varepsilon)\big|^2\,\delta\big(\varepsilon-\varepsilon_{n'k'}\big) \;\;\Longrightarrow\;\; \frac{1}{\tau^{\rm qp}_{nk}}=\frac{2\pi}{\hbar}\,c\,N_k\sum_{n'k'}\big|T_{nk,n'k'}(\varepsilon_{nk})\big|^2\,\delta\big(\varepsilon_{nk}-\varepsilon_{n'k'}\big).$$

Because the defect is static the scattering is elastic: only the constant-energy contour of $\varepsilon_{nk}$ contributes, and the temperature enters transport solely through occupation factors.

A.2  Quasiparticle rate vs. transport rate

Forward scattering degrades current much less than backscattering. The momentum-relaxation (transport) rate weights each event by the relative velocity loss:

$$\boxed{\;\frac{1}{\tau^{\rm tr}_{nk}}=\frac{2\pi}{\hbar}\,c\,N_k\sum_{n'k'}\big|T_{nk,n'k'}(\varepsilon_{nk})\big|^2\, \delta\big(\varepsilon_{nk}-\varepsilon_{n'k'}\big)\left(1-\frac{\mathbf v_{nk}\!\cdot\!\mathbf v_{n'k'}}{|\mathbf v_{nk}|\,|\mathbf v_{n'k'}|}\right)\;}$$

with band velocities $\mathbf v_{nk}=\hbar^{-1}\nabla_k\varepsilon_{nk}$ (from the same Wannier $H(R)$ used in §9: $\mathbf v$ is the gauge-covariant $\partial_k H_W$ sandwich). The $(1-\cos\theta)$ form is exact for isotropic dispersions; the general anisotropic statement is the linearized BTE below. Near a $T$-matrix resonance (a defect level grazing a band edge, e.g. our O$_S$ band-edge doublets) $|T|^2$ is strongly enhanced and strongly angle-dependent — exactly the regime where using the full $T$ instead of the Born approximation matters.

A.3  RTA mobility, and the exact linearized BTE

In the relaxation-time approximation the dc conductivity tensor (2D, spin degeneracy $g_s{=}2$, sample area $A$) and mobility are

$$\sigma_{\alpha\beta}= \frac{g_s e^2}{A}\sum_{nk}\tau^{\rm tr}_{nk}\,v^{\alpha}_{nk}v^{\beta}_{nk}\left(-\frac{\partial f}{\partial\varepsilon}\right)_{\!\varepsilon_{nk}}, \qquad \mu_{\alpha\beta}=\frac{\sigma_{\alpha\beta}}{e\,n_c},\qquad n_c=\frac{g_s}{A}\sum_{nk} f(\varepsilon_{nk})\ \ (\text{electrons; analogous for holes}),$$

with $f$ the Fermi function at temperature $T$ and chemical potential $\mu_c$ (set by the doping level). Since impurity scattering is elastic, $\mu(T)$ varies only through $(-\partial f/\partial\varepsilon)$ sampling the energy dependence of $\tau^{\rm tr}(\varepsilon)$ — strong $T$-dependence is a fingerprint of resonances near the band edge.

The $\tau^{\rm tr}$ shortcut is exact only for isotropic dispersions and isotropic-in-angle $|T|^2$. The exact treatment is the iterative BTE below.

A.4  Iterative BTE (IBTE): the exact solution for elastic scattering

Linearize the steady-state BTE with the ansatz $\delta f_{nk}=e\left(-\partial f^0/\partial\varepsilon\right)\,\mathbf E\!\cdot\!\mathbf F_{nk}$, where $\mathbf F_{nk}$ is the vector mean free path. For elastic defect scattering the collision kernel is the on-shell golden-rule matrix

$$P_{nk\to n'k'}=\frac{2\pi}{\hbar}\,c\,N_k\,\big|T_{nk,n'k'}(\varepsilon_{nk})\big|^2\,\delta(\varepsilon_{nk}-\varepsilon_{n'k'}) \;=\;P_{n'k'\to nk}\quad(\text{symmetric on shell}),$$

and the linearized equation becomes a fixed-point problem:

$$\boxed{\;\mathbf F_{nk}\;=\;\tau^{\rm qp}_{nk}\,\mathbf v_{nk}\;+\;\tau^{\rm qp}_{nk}\sum_{n'k'}P_{nk\to n'k'}\,\mathbf F_{n'k'}\;}, \qquad \frac{1}{\tau^{\rm qp}_{nk}}=\sum_{n'k'}P_{nk\to n'k'},$$
$$\sigma_{\alpha\beta}=\frac{g_s e^2}{A}\sum_{nk}\left(-\frac{\partial f}{\partial\varepsilon}\right)_{\!\varepsilon_{nk}} v^{\alpha}_{nk}\,F^{\beta}_{nk}.$$

Iteration and convergence. Start from the RTA seed $\mathbf F^{(0)}=\tau^{\rm qp}\mathbf v$ (this zeroth iterate is the SERTA/qp-RTA result) and iterate $\mathbf F^{(i+1)}=\tau^{\rm qp}\mathbf v+\tau^{\rm qp}P\,\mathbf F^{(i)}$. This is the Neumann series of $\big[\mathbb 1-\tau^{\rm qp}P\big]^{-1}$; because the collision operator $C=\mathrm{diag}(1/\tau^{\rm qp})-P$ is symmetric positive semidefinite (its only null vector, the uniform shift, carries no current), the series converges geometrically — typically a dozen iterations. The physics of the second term is in-scattering backflow: carriers scattered from $k'$ into $k$ retain forward momentum memory, which RTA discards; the first iteration beyond RTA already recovers most of the $(1-\cos\theta)$ physics, and the fixed point recovers it exactly.

Structure worth exploiting. Elasticity block-diagonalizes the problem on iso-energy contours: $P$ only connects states with $\varepsilon_{nk}=\varepsilon_{n'k'}$, so on a discretized contour the fixed point can equally be obtained by a direct solve of the small linear system $C\mathbf F=\mathbf v$ per energy — on our grids this is trivial. (The practical crux is regularizing $\delta(\varepsilon-\varepsilon')$ on a discrete grid: adaptive Gaussian smearing or tetrahedron integration on the elastic contour.)

Exactness and hierarchy. For dilute elastic scatterers the linearized-BTE fixed point is the exact semiclassical answer at every anisotropy: $\;\text{SERTA }(\tau^{\rm qp})\;\subset\;\text{tr-RTA }(\tau^{\rm tr},\ 1-\cos\theta)\;\subset\;\text{IBTE (exact)}$. For our band-edge $T$-matrix resonances (O$_S$) the kernel $|T_{kk'}|^2$ is strongly energy- and angle-structured, so the three levels can differ materially — IBTE is the one to trust. Finally, substituting $\mathbf F_{nk}=\tau^{\rm qp}_{nk}\tilde{\mathbf v}_{nk}$ maps the fixed-point equation term by term onto the on-shell limit of the Kubo ladder equation of §B.2: IBTE $\equiv$ Kubo$+$ladder — the two "exact" endpoints of routes A and B are the same theory. EDI's transport module already carries the iterative_bte input scaffolding for exactly this kernel.

B · Route 2: Kubo formula on the spectral function

B.1  Kubo–Greenwood bubble

Linear response gives the dc conductivity as the current–current correlator. Keeping the disorder-averaged Green functions and the bare velocity vertex (the "bubble"), the dc Kubo–Greenwood formula reads

$$\boxed{\;\sigma^{\rm bubble}_{\alpha\beta} =\frac{g_s\,\pi\hbar e^2}{A}\int\! d\omega\left(-\frac{\partial f}{\partial\omega}\right) \sum_{k}\,{\rm Tr}\Big[\,\hat v^{\alpha}_k\,\hat A(k,\omega)\,\hat v^{\beta}_k\,\hat A(k,\omega)\Big]\;}$$

where $\hat A(k,\omega)=\frac{i}{2\pi}\big[\bar G^R-\bar G^A\big]$ and $\hat v^\alpha_k$ are matrices in the band index (interband elements included). If the spectral operator is band-diagonal — a good approximation when $\Gamma$ is small against band splittings — this collapses to the intraband form

$$\sigma^{\rm bubble}_{\alpha\beta}\simeq\frac{g_s\,\pi\hbar e^2}{A}\int\! d\omega\left(-\frac{\partial f}{\partial\omega}\right)\sum_{nk} v^{\alpha}_{nk}v^{\beta}_{nk}\,\big[A_n(k,\omega)\big]^2,$$

i.e. the square of exactly the $A(k,\omega)$ computed in §9 (with $\eta\to0^+$, the physical $\Gamma$ doing the broadening). For a Lorentzian $A_n$ the $\omega$-integral gives $\int A_n^2\,d\omega = 1/(\pi\Gamma_{nk})=\tau^{\rm qp}_{nk}/(\pi\hbar)$, and the bubble collapses to the RTA expression with the quasiparticle time: $\sigma^{\rm bubble}\to \frac{g_se^2}{A}\sum \tau^{\rm qp}v^\alpha v^\beta(-\partial f/\partial\varepsilon)$. The bubble alone therefore overestimates scattering's effect on nothing — rather it misses the forward-scattering correction: it knows $\tau^{\rm qp}$, not $\tau^{\rm tr}$.

B.2  Vertex corrections: the ladder restores $(1-\cos\theta)$

Dressing one velocity vertex with the ladder of impurity lines (the diagrams consistent with the non-crossing self-energy — a Ward-identity requirement) replaces $v\to\tilde v$ solving

$$\tilde v^{\alpha}_{nk}(\omega)=v^{\alpha}_{nk}+c\,N_k\sum_{n'k'}\big|T_{nk,n'k'}(\omega)\big|^2\,\bar G^R_{n'}(k',\omega)\bar G^A_{n'}(k',\omega)\,\tilde v^{\alpha}_{n'k'}(\omega),$$

and the standard result (Mahan, Rammer) is that Kubo + ladder $\equiv$ linearized BTE for dilute elastic scatterers: the geometric series of $\bar G^R\bar G^A\,|T|^2$ generates exactly the $(1-\cos\theta)$ weight, turning $\tau^{\rm qp}$ into $\tau^{\rm tr}$. Diagrams beyond the ladder (maximally crossed) add weak localization — a quantum correction outside the BTE, relevant at low $T$ in 2D.

E.4  Finalized numbers: joint $\eta\to0$, $N_{\rm int}\to\infty$ ladder extrapolation

Ladder design. The elastic-contour discretization has two coupled knobs: the internal torus $N_{\rm int}^2$ (level spacing) and the Lorentzian width $\eta$ (which must track the spacing from above). They are taken to their joint limit along the ladder $\eta = 600/N_{\rm int}$ meV with rungs $N_{\rm int}=24,36,48,60$ ($\eta=25,16.7,12.5,10$ meV), each side at its physical pin ($\omega_0$=VBM for holes, CBM for electrons); $\mu(N)$ is then extrapolated linearly in $1/N\propto\eta$ over the last three rungs. A fixed-$N$ crosscheck ($N=48$: $\eta$ 25$\to$12.5 meV) isolates the pure-$\eta$ slope: for holes it accounts for most of the ladder trend ($-12\%$ at 300 K, $-29\%$ at 100 K), while for electrons it is nearly flat ($0$–$4\%$) — the hole ladder is $\eta$-dominated (kernel sharpening), the electron ladder grid-dominated (K-pocket sampling).

Two solver hardenings were forced by the $\eta\to0$ limit (both caught by assertions, not by luck): (i) the optical theorem pins the row sums of $\tau P$ at $\approx$1, so the even, per-energy-shell conservation modes approach eigenvalue 1 as window leakage shrinks and cross it through symmetrization noise ($\lambda_{\max}=1.003$–$1.011$ at $N\ge36$) — the direct resolvent $[1-\tau P]^{-1}$ becomes invalid. The IBTE is instead solved by fixed-point iteration with per-energy-bin mean deflation: the physical $F$ is odd in $k$, so every bin mean vanishes identically (gauge-safe — bin sums are traces over degenerate doublets); the deflation leaves the solution invariant while exactly removing the $\lambda\to1$ modes. Convergence to $10^{-11}$ in 104–451 sweeps; where the direct solve is still valid ($\lambda_{\max}<0.995$) the two agree to $7\times10^{-4}$. (ii) At small $\eta$ the interior window admits band-extremum states ($v\to0$ at K, present on every $3\,|\,N$ grid), whose $\tau_{\rm IBTE}=\mathbf F\!\cdot\!\mathbf v/|\mathbf v|^2$ is a 0/0; they poison the $r(\varepsilon)$ parameterization (caught by the $\mu_{xx}/\mu_{yy}$ isotropy assertion) and are excluded by a velocity floor ($v>5\%$ of the window median) — legitimately, since their transport weight $\propto v^2$ vanishes.

E.5  On-shell $\Sigma_n(k)$ along $\Gamma$–K–M–$\Gamma$ — and an exact low-rank solver

The $k$-resolved anatomy of everything above: the on-shell diagonal self-energy $\Sigma_n(k)=c\,N_k\,T_{nn}(k,\varepsilon_{nk}+i\eta)$ for the highest valence and lowest conduction band, each with its physical downfolding pin ($\omega_0$=VBM / CBM), solved exactly on shell at every one of the 286 path points (no energy binning), $\eta=16.7$ meV. Production settings after the hardening below: character-tracked band sheets (greedy max-overlap along the path; kills the identity-switch jumps of energy-sorted indexing — the CB doublet is exactly degenerate at $\Gamma$, and V$_{\rm S}$ has a 14 meV avoided crossing near the Q valley) on a $96^2$ torus (affordable at sub-second per $\omega$ only because of the low-rank solver below), with the $36^2$ curves kept as convergence ghosts. V$_{\rm S}$ uses the regauged v5 vertex (§E.6).

The exact low-rank (Woodbury) solver. The densified vertex is interpolative by construction, $\tilde V_{\rm fine}=\tilde L\,V_c\,\tilde L^\dagger$ with $\tilde L=L\otimes\mathbb 1_{11}$, so ${\rm rank}\,\tilde V_{\rm fine}\le 1584$ (the coarse-grid dimension) algebraically, however dense the torus. The Dyson series then folds exactly onto the coarse space:

$$\tau(\omega)=\bigl[1-V_c\,\tilde g(\omega)\bigr]^{-1}V_c,\qquad \tilde g(\omega)=\tfrac{N_k}{N_{\rm int}^2}\,\Phi^\dagger\,{\rm diag}\!\Bigl(\tfrac{1}{\omega-\varepsilon_{qn}+i\eta}\Bigr)\,\Phi,\qquad \Sigma_n(k_p)=c\,N_k\,v^\dagger\tau\,v,$$

with $\Phi_{(qn),(jb)}=L_{qj}\,C^*_{q,bn}$ precomputed once and $v$ an interpolation-row $\times$ band-vector outer product. Eigen-truncating $V_c$ at $10^{-9}$ relative gives effective ranks 1026/1011 (O$_S$) and 865/866 (V$_{\rm S}$ — the vacancy vertex is the more local one), and the per-$\omega$ cost drops from a dense $O(N_{\rm int}^6)$ LU to one $r^2\times(N_{\rm int}^2\cdot 11)$ GEMM $+$ an $r^3$ solve: 210 ms vs 9 s per $\omega$ at $36^2$ (40$\times$), and linear instead of cubic in torus size ($60^2$: sub-second vs 5–6 min). The same factorization retrofits the §E.4 IBTE ladder — the window projection satisfies $T_{\rm win}=\Phi_{\rm win}\,\tau\,\Phi_{\rm win}^\dagger$ — making future $N=72/96$ rungs cheap.

Validation, and a lesson. Binned-mode A/B against the dense-LU reference: max $|\Delta\Sigma|=0.001/0.002$ μeV across both bands and both defects — machine-precision equivalence of the factorized and dense solvers. The A/B gate also caught a real bug on first flight: the $V_c$ eigenbasis rotation used $U^{T}$ instead of $U^\dagger$, scrambling $\Sigma$ by $\sim$30% while passing the causality assertion — the anti-hermitian part of $\tau$ is negative-definite for any quadratic form, so ${\rm Im}\,\Sigma\le0$ cannot detect basis-rotation errors. Only a pin-to-reference A/B can; it is now a permanent fixture of the pipeline. The ragged first version of this figure then led one level deeper — to a genuine gauge-provenance bug in the V$_{\rm S}$ data itself (§E.6).

E.6  Errata: the V$_{\rm S}$ gauge-provenance bug — found, fixed, and triple-validated

What was wrong. The V$_{\rm S}$ vertex (edi_direct_v4) had been computed against the wavefunctions of an earlier NSCF run than the one the Wannier $U(k)$ matrices were generated from. Two independent diagonalizations give random per-$(k,{\rm band})$ phases and arbitrary mixes within degenerate subspaces, so the data's Bloch gauge was incoherent with $U(k)$. Everything $k$-diagonal is immune (per-$k$ phases cancel): all raw-basis diagonalizations, defect levels, DOS, supercell benchmarks and $|T|^2$ moduli at data points — every published V$_{\rm S}$ level/DOS result stands. What breaks is coherent-across-$k$ machinery: Wannier interpolation and densification. The R-space fingerprint is decisive: the wrong-gauge vertex cannot localize (flat $\sim$$10^{-3}$ eV shells, no peak), while the regauged one focuses onto the defect cell (shell peak $9.6\times10^{-2}$ eV at $d{=}3$, floor/peak $1.8\times10^{-3}$).

The fix, cross-validated on two machines. The vertex was recomputed against the correct wavefunctions independently on a second cluster (Anvil; AOCC + reference LAPACK + FFTW3 vs Banff's ifx + MKL + DFTI). Three closure layers: (i) gauge-immune invariants (diagonal traces, per-block Frobenius norms) agree with the old data to $10^{-11}$–$10^{-13}$ — proving the physical content was identical and only the gauge differed; (ii) element-wise cross-compiler A/B on all $1.0\times10^8$ matrix elements: max $3.8\times10^{-14}$ Ry — machine precision across fully heterogeneous stacks; (iii) an independent end-to-end pipeline (own SCF $\to$ NSCF $\to$ wannier90 $\to$ vertex) reproduces the Wannier spread to 5 ppm, with the final vertex comparison in flight.

Impact on numbers. The gauge noise acted as fake incoherent scattering with side-dependent sign: it suppressed V$_{\rm S}$ hole mobility by $\sim$50% (300 K ladder 98/113/116 $\to$ 150/165/180; extrapolated 135 $\to$ 207) and inflated V$_{\rm S}$ electron mobility by 35–40% (113/141 regauged vs 171/234) — diluting the real K-valley channels into weak uniform background weakens the resummed backscattering. O$_S$ is untouched (same-provenance throughout). All V$_{\rm S}$ tables above now carry the regauged values.

E.7  The Wannier-basis Koster–Slater engine: $\eta\to0^+$ directly, no extrapolation

Structural upgrade. The downfolded vertex is static and local, so the Dyson equation can be solved inside its own support: $\tau(\omega)=[\mathbb 1-v\,\tilde g(\omega)]^{-1}v$, with $v$ the $1584$-dimensional coarse-grid vertex (unitarily the $(R,R')$ Wannier-pair representation; effective rank $\sim$1000 at $10^{-9}$), and all $\omega$-dependence in the projected host lattice Green function $\tilde g(\omega)$. The dense $k$-torus of §E.1–E.4 is thereby demoted from “the space the Dyson equation is discretized on” to a mere quadrature for the analytically $k$-diagonal host — evaluated once as a centroid-corrected spectral density (1 meV bins inside the transport windows, 25 meV elsewhere, $480^2$ sampling), after which any $(\omega,\eta)$ costs one batched GEMM: 135 ms per $\omega$. The two errors the §E.4 ladder had to extrapolate jointly — host-propagator discreteness and kernel discreteness — decouple: the former is simply converged away, the latter remains as a single clean limit (elastic-kernel grid $N_W$ with matched $\eta=600/N_W$ meV, rungs 96/144/192, i.e. $\eta$ down to 3.1 meV vs the old floor of 10).

One more assertion-caught lesson. Feeding $\eta$=2 meV rates into a kernel sampled on a $48^2$ window grid violated the optical theorem by 170% (Lorentzian comb undersampling) and, more subtly, quadrature error at sharp resonances pushes some kernel row sums above the exact out-rate — even-sector eigenvalues of $\tau P$ then exceed 1 and no damping can fix a real $\lambda>1$ (the V$_{\rm S}$ electron solve stalled exactly so). The cure is physical: enforce the optical theorem on the kernel (row-rescale to $\Gamma_s/\hbar$, re-symmetrize; residual deviation 0.4–0.9% median), after which every configuration converges.

G · Kubo mobility computed: full spectral lineshapes on the transport window (all-SG15)

Route B (§B) taken to production for all three defects (SG15 arms throughout), using the same self-energy object as the transport pipeline: $\Sigma_s(\omega)=cN_k\,T_{ss}(\omega)$ from the Wannier Koster–Slater engine, and the structurally-positive Dyson spectral function $A_s(\omega)=-\tfrac1\pi{\rm Im}[\omega-\varepsilon_s- \Sigma_s(\omega)+i0^+]^{-1}$ (§F.3 construction). Two tiers per carrier:

K1 (QP-Lorentzian): $\tau_s=\hbar/\Gamma_s$, $\Gamma_s=2|{\rm Im}\Sigma_s(\varepsilon_s)|$ — analytically $\equiv$ SERTA via $\int A^2 d\omega=1/(\pi\Gamma)$. K2 (full lineshape): $\mu\propto\sum_s v_s^2\,\pi\hbar\!\int\!d\omega\,(-\partial_\omega f)\,A_s(\omega)^2$ — the Fermi weight sits at $\omega$, where the spectral weight actually is, not at the bare band energy. Parameters: $300^2$ state grid, $\omega$-grid 0.2 meV (2701 points/carrier, one $r{\times}r$ T-solve + projection GEMM per $\omega$, $r\approx10^3$), $\eta$=1 meV on the $480^2$ continuum host, $n_c=10^{12}$ cm$^{-2}$, $c$=1/36. The hierarchy, resolved. There is no universal “Kubo > BTE”: the exact statement is bubble $\le$ bubble+ladder ($\equiv$ IBTE), while bubble-vs-SERTA is set by the sign of Re$\Sigma$ (which way the weight moves relative to $\mu$). Our data realizes both textbook limits at once: in the clean cells (Re$\Sigma>0$, weight pushed into the band) K2$_{\rm band}$ < SERTA < IBTE; in the band-tail cells (weight pulled toward/into the gap) K2$_{\rm full}$ explodes — the classic “Kubo exceeds BTE” regime, here diagnosed as velocity-bookkeeping artifact for bound-like weight. The two corrections are independent and opposite, giving the sandwich $$\mu_{\rm full\ Kubo}\;\approx\;\mu_{\rm IBTE}\times e^{-{\rm Re}\Sigma/k_BT} \;\;\in\;[{\rm K2}_{\rm band},\,\mu_{\rm IBTE}]$$ — for Se$_S$ electrons at 300 K: $\approx1109\times0.57\approx630$ cm$^2$/V·s, between 448 and 1109. Closing the sandwich exactly (Kubo + ladder with full lineshapes) is the natural next rung.
