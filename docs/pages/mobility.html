Defect-Limited Carrier Mobility from the $T$-matrix

Two routes from the machinery of this site to electron/hole mobility: (A) the Boltzmann transport equation with scattering rates from the on-shell imaginary self-energy, and (B) the Kubo formula evaluated directly on the disorder-averaged spectral function $A(k,\omega)$. Formulas and their derivation sketches only — no numerical implementation yet.

0 · Setup: from one impurity to a dilute random array

Everything on this site so far is single-impurity: the vertex $\tilde V(k_i,k_f)=W_{PP}+W_{PQ}[\omega_0-H_{QQ}]^{-1}W_{QP}$ (A1-augmented, artifact-free) and the torus $T$-matrix $T(k,k';\omega)=\big[1-\tilde V G^0(\omega)\big]^{-1}\tilde V$ on the $N_k$-cell supercell. Transport needs the disorder-averaged Green function for a random array of identical defects with concentration $c$ per primitive cell (areal density $n_{\rm imp}=c/A_{\rm cell}$; our supercells correspond to $c=1/36$). To lowest order in $c$ (dilute, non-overlapping impurities — the standard non-crossing $T$-matrix approximation of disorder theory):

$$\boxed{\;\Sigma_{n}(k,\omega)\;=\;c\,N_k\,T_{nk,\,nk}(\omega)\;+\;\mathcal O(c^2)\;}$$

Our torus $T$ scales as $1/N_k$ (because $\tilde V=M/N_k$), so $c\,N_k\,T$ is finite and $N_k$-independent in the thermodynamic limit — the combination $N_kT$ is the physical per-impurity scattering amplitude, and $c$ counts impurities. The averaged retarded Green function and spectral function are then

$$\bar G^R_{n}(k,\omega)=\frac{1}{\omega-\varepsilon_{nk}-\Sigma_n(k,\omega)},\qquad A_n(k,\omega)=-\tfrac1\pi\,{\rm Im}\,\bar G^R_n(k,\omega) =\frac{1}{\pi}\,\frac{\Gamma_{nk}(\omega)/2}{\big[\omega-\varepsilon_{nk}-{\rm Re}\,\Sigma\big]^2+\big[\Gamma_{nk}(\omega)/2\big]^2},$$

with the (energy-dependent) broadening $\Gamma_{nk}(\omega)=-2\,{\rm Im}\,\Sigma_n(k,\omega)\ge0$. Two immediate connections to what is already computed: (i) the maps of §9 of the Augmentation page are exactly $A_n(k,\omega)$ at $c=1/N_k$ (one impurity per torus) plus a visualization broadening $\eta$; (ii) the linewidth of a quasiparticle peak in those maps, minus $\eta$, is the scattering rate scaled to that concentration. For transport the $\eta$ used for plotting must never enter — only the physical $\Gamma$ does.

A · Route 1: Boltzmann transport with $T$-matrix rates

A.1  On-shell rate: "Im $\Sigma$ = scattering rate × factor"

The quasiparticle (out-scattering) lifetime follows from evaluating $\Sigma$ on shell, $\omega\to\varepsilon_{nk}$:

$$\boxed{\;\frac{1}{\tau^{\rm qp}_{nk}}\;=\;\frac{2}{\hbar}\,\big|{\rm Im}\,\Sigma_n(k,\varepsilon_{nk})\big| \;=\;\frac{2\,c\,N_k}{\hbar}\,\big|{\rm Im}\,T_{nk,nk}(\varepsilon_{nk}+i0^+)\big|\;}$$

— the "factor" is precisely $2/\hbar$ (lifetime vs. amplitude decay) together with the concentration $cN_k$ hidden inside $\Sigma$. The generalized optical theorem turns the diagonal Im $T$ into an explicit golden rule with the full $T$ replacing the bare vertex (all scattering orders, exact for one impurity):

$${\rm Im}\,T_{nk,nk}(\varepsilon+i0^+)=-\pi\sum_{n'k'}\big|T_{nk,\,n'k'}(\varepsilon)\big|^2\,\delta\big(\varepsilon-\varepsilon_{n'k'}\big) \;\;\Longrightarrow\;\; \frac{1}{\tau^{\rm qp}_{nk}}=\frac{2\pi}{\hbar}\,c\,N_k\sum_{n'k'}\big|T_{nk,n'k'}(\varepsilon_{nk})\big|^2\,\delta\big(\varepsilon_{nk}-\varepsilon_{n'k'}\big).$$

Because the defect is static the scattering is elastic: only the constant-energy contour of $\varepsilon_{nk}$ contributes, and the temperature enters transport solely through occupation factors.

A.2  Quasiparticle rate vs. transport rate

Forward scattering degrades current much less than backscattering. The momentum-relaxation (transport) rate weights each event by the relative velocity loss:

$$\boxed{\;\frac{1}{\tau^{\rm tr}_{nk}}=\frac{2\pi}{\hbar}\,c\,N_k\sum_{n'k'}\big|T_{nk,n'k'}(\varepsilon_{nk})\big|^2\, \delta\big(\varepsilon_{nk}-\varepsilon_{n'k'}\big)\left(1-\frac{\mathbf v_{nk}\!\cdot\!\mathbf v_{n'k'}}{|\mathbf v_{nk}|\,|\mathbf v_{n'k'}|}\right)\;}$$

with band velocities $\mathbf v_{nk}=\hbar^{-1}\nabla_k\varepsilon_{nk}$ (from the same Wannier $H(R)$ used in §9: $\mathbf v$ is the gauge-covariant $\partial_k H_W$ sandwich). The $(1-\cos\theta)$ form is exact for isotropic dispersions; the general anisotropic statement is the linearized BTE below. Near a $T$-matrix resonance (a defect level grazing a band edge, e.g. our O$_S$ band-edge doublets) $|T|^2$ is strongly enhanced and strongly angle-dependent — exactly the regime where using the full $T$ instead of the Born approximation matters.

A.3  RTA mobility, and the exact linearized BTE

In the relaxation-time approximation the dc conductivity tensor (2D, spin degeneracy $g_s{=}2$, sample area $A$) and mobility are

$$\sigma_{\alpha\beta}= \frac{g_s e^2}{A}\sum_{nk}\tau^{\rm tr}_{nk}\,v^{\alpha}_{nk}v^{\beta}_{nk}\left(-\frac{\partial f}{\partial\varepsilon}\right)_{\!\varepsilon_{nk}}, \qquad \mu_{\alpha\beta}=\frac{\sigma_{\alpha\beta}}{e\,n_c},\qquad n_c=\frac{g_s}{A}\sum_{nk} f(\varepsilon_{nk})\ \ (\text{electrons; analogous for holes}),$$

with $f$ the Fermi function at temperature $T$ and chemical potential $\mu_c$ (set by the doping level). Since impurity scattering is elastic, $\mu(T)$ varies only through $(-\partial f/\partial\varepsilon)$ sampling the energy dependence of $\tau^{\rm tr}(\varepsilon)$ — strong $T$-dependence is a fingerprint of resonances near the band edge.

Beyond RTA, the linearized steady-state BTE for the deviation $\delta f_{nk}=f-f^0$ under field $\mathbf E$ is solved exactly (for elastic scattering) by iterating

$$-e\,\mathbf E\!\cdot\!\mathbf v_{nk}\left(-\frac{\partial f}{\partial\varepsilon}\right)=\sum_{n'k'}P_{nk\to n'k'}\big(\delta f_{nk}-\delta f_{n'k'}\big), \qquad P_{nk\to n'k'}=\frac{2\pi}{\hbar}\,c\,N_k\big|T_{nk,n'k'}\big|^2\delta(\varepsilon_{nk}-\varepsilon_{n'k'}),$$

which reduces to the $\tau^{\rm tr}$ form for isotropic bands and is the gold standard otherwise (EDI's transport module already carries the iterative_bte scaffolding for exactly this kernel).

B · Route 2: Kubo formula on the spectral function

B.1  Kubo–Greenwood bubble

Linear response gives the dc conductivity as the current–current correlator. Keeping the disorder-averaged Green functions and the bare velocity vertex (the "bubble"), the dc Kubo–Greenwood formula reads

$$\boxed{\;\sigma^{\rm bubble}_{\alpha\beta} =\frac{g_s\,\pi\hbar e^2}{A}\int\! d\omega\left(-\frac{\partial f}{\partial\omega}\right) \sum_{k}\,{\rm Tr}\Big[\,\hat v^{\alpha}_k\,\hat A(k,\omega)\,\hat v^{\beta}_k\,\hat A(k,\omega)\Big]\;}$$

where $\hat A(k,\omega)=\frac{i}{2\pi}\big[\bar G^R-\bar G^A\big]$ and $\hat v^\alpha_k$ are matrices in the band index (interband elements included). If the spectral operator is band-diagonal — a good approximation when $\Gamma$ is small against band splittings — this collapses to the intraband form

$$\sigma^{\rm bubble}_{\alpha\beta}\simeq\frac{g_s\,\pi\hbar e^2}{A}\int\! d\omega\left(-\frac{\partial f}{\partial\omega}\right)\sum_{nk} v^{\alpha}_{nk}v^{\beta}_{nk}\,\big[A_n(k,\omega)\big]^2,$$

i.e. the square of exactly the $A(k,\omega)$ computed in §9 (with $\eta\to0^+$, the physical $\Gamma$ doing the broadening). For a Lorentzian $A_n$ the $\omega$-integral gives $\int A_n^2\,d\omega = 1/(\pi\Gamma_{nk})=\tau^{\rm qp}_{nk}/(\pi\hbar)$, and the bubble collapses to the RTA expression with the quasiparticle time: $\sigma^{\rm bubble}\to \frac{g_se^2}{A}\sum \tau^{\rm qp}v^\alpha v^\beta(-\partial f/\partial\varepsilon)$. The bubble alone therefore overestimates scattering's effect on nothing — rather it misses the forward-scattering correction: it knows $\tau^{\rm qp}$, not $\tau^{\rm tr}$.

B.2  Vertex corrections: the ladder restores $(1-\cos\theta)$

Dressing one velocity vertex with the ladder of impurity lines (the diagrams consistent with the non-crossing self-energy — a Ward-identity requirement) replaces $v\to\tilde v$ solving

$$\tilde v^{\alpha}_{nk}(\omega)=v^{\alpha}_{nk}+c\,N_k\sum_{n'k'}\big|T_{nk,n'k'}(\omega)\big|^2\,\bar G^R_{n'}(k',\omega)\bar G^A_{n'}(k',\omega)\,\tilde v^{\alpha}_{n'k'}(\omega),$$

and the standard result (Mahan, Rammer) is that Kubo + ladder $\equiv$ linearized BTE for dilute elastic scatterers: the geometric series of $\bar G^R\bar G^A\,|T|^2$ generates exactly the $(1-\cos\theta)$ weight, turning $\tau^{\rm qp}$ into $\tau^{\rm tr}$. Diagrams beyond the ladder (maximally crossed) add weak localization — a quantum correction outside the BTE, relevant at low $T$ in 2D.
